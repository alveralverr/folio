AI Slackbot

Overview

As the digital space becomes an integral part of our daily lives, it also provides a platform for toxic language and abusive behaviour.[1] Our Sydney-based client, a certified social trader organisation specialising in aiding enterprises on DEI, wanted a working prototype that addresses misogyny and sexism in online communication to demonstrate to their customers.

This project utilizes a large language model (LLM) to aid content moderation in minimizing the harmful effects of misogyny and sexism in workplace group chats.


The final output is an AI-powered chatbot integrated in a Slack channel which provides real-time feedback in helping workplace conversations become more inclusive.

Due to request for confidentiality, some technical details of this project cannot be disclosed.

Problem

  • Misogyny (derogatory comments or sexual innuendo) is still widespread [1].
  • This may result in a hostile work environment leading to stress, isolation, and reduced productivity or job satisfaction[2].
  • Messaging platforms lack built-in moderation as a mechanism against toxic behaviour.
  • Manual content moderation can be slow, time-consuming, and emotionally burdensome.

Motivation
  • People work better in teams where they feel safe.
  • Diverse teams can achieve more.

Vision Mission

To develop a solution that empowers its users so that companies can benefit from a more inclusive work atmosphere without misogyny and sexism.

Implement a faster, more efficient, and arguably more impartial content moderation tool in the workplace by leveraging GenAI technology.

Target Users

Employees, HR Departments, IT Teams, Team Leads, Managers and Owners


Aims

The goal is education-based impact towards the long-term behavioural change of its users, rather than surveillance and censorship.


Impact

This first prototype is currently being used for further development and seeking of funding, based on the last correspondence with the client.


Deliverables

  1. Minimum Viable Product (MVP)
    • recognize misogynistic text (true or false)
    • 75-80% accuracy on a balanced dataset
    • collecting misogynistic text in a file
    • can be run locally on the terminal
  2. Choice of LLM, messaging app, and dataset for evaluation
    • decision-making backed by literature review, user and market research
  3. Final Prototype
    • additional features
    • AI chatbot integration in Slack
    • Deployed on the web (at the time of submission)
  4. Documentation
    • GitHub repo with source code and readme

Final Product

Current Capabilities The Chatbot can detect even subtle misogyny by considering the context of conversations. When a potentially misogynistic message is flagged, the user receives a direct message with: The flagged message A probability score A brief explanation of why the message might be considered misogynistic HR Private Channel Additional Features HR Management Tools Statistics on individuals and teams are available as a CSV file. Notifications are sent to a private Slack channel for HR to ensure important information is not overlooked. Realtime Evaluation Developed a Chrome extension for live message evaluation. Implemented a traffic light system (red, orange, green) to visualize the evaluation and probability scores for users.
